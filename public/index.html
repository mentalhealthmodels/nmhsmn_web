Using systems modelling for evidence-informed mental health reform in Australia.

By Caroline Gao on August 27, 2022

To introduce the National Mental Health Systems Modelling Network, members pooled their expertise to draft an article about the role of systems modelling in Australian mental health reform processes. The article provides an overview of current and previous mental health reform plans in Australia, identifies and describes different modelling approaches and the types of topics they are particularly well suited to addressing and discusses challenges and opportunities for planners and policymakers relating to the commissioning, development and use of model based tools.

Australian Mental Health Systems Models Dataverse

By Matthew Hamilton on August 22, 2022

To facilitate the sharing of model data by the Australian mental health modelling community, our network curates an open access data repository. The data repository is the Australian Mental Health Systems Models Dataverse. If you have data from a mental health modelling project that you would like to share via this repository, check out this tutorial about how to do so.

Australian Mental Health Systems Models Zenodo Community

By Matthew Hamilton on August 22, 2022

To facilitate the sharing of model code by the Australian mental health modelling community, our network curates an open access code repository. The code repository is the Australian Mental Health Systems Models Zenodo Community. Linked to this repository is the network’s GitHub organisation. Currently our GitHub organisation is just used to host the source code for this website, but over time we aim to use it to share brief code snippets that may not be appropriate for archiving in our Zenodo repository.
